Description

A hamburger is a popular sandwich consisting of a seasoned ground beef patty cooked and served inside a sliced bun,
typically accompanied by toppings such as lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, cheese, ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise.
It’s widely enjoyed as a casual, handheld meal and can be customized in countless ways, with variations in bun types,
condiments, and cooking methods.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Cheddar cheese
  • Hamburger buns
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Pickles
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard
  • Mayonnaise
  • Butter

Instructions

  1. Form the Patties: Divide 1 pound of ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio) into 4 equal portions and gently shape into round patties, about ¾ inch thick.
  2. Season the Patties: Sprinkle both sides with salt and pepper just before cooking.
  3. Preheat Your Cooking Surface: Heat a skillet, grill, or griddle over medium-high heat until hot.
  4. Cook the Patties: Place patties on the hot surface and cook 3–4 minutes per side (or to desired doneness), flipping once.
  5. Add Cheese (Optional): Place a slice of cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.
  6. Toast the Buns: Lightly butter the insides of the hamburger buns and toast them on the skillet or grill until golden.
  7. Assemble the Burger: Place the cooked patty on the bottom bun, then layer with desired toppings (e.g., lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles).
  8. Add Condiments: Spread ketchup, mustard, mayo, or other sauces on the top bun.
  9. Top and Serve: Close the burger with the top bun.
  10. Enjoy: Serve hot with fries, chips, or a side of your choice.
