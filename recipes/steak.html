Description

A grilled steak is a thick, juicy cut of beef cooked over an open flame or hot grill to develop a flavorful seared crust while
keeping the inside tender and cooked to your preferred doneness. Often seasoned simply with salt and pepper, steak can be
enjoyed on its own or paired with sauces, sides, or vegetables for a hearty, protein-rich meal.

Ingredients

  • Steak (ribeye, sirloin, strip, or filet)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic (optional)
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)
  • Butter (optional, for finishing)

Instructions

  1. Choose Your Cut: Select a steak cut like ribeye, sirloin, NY strip, or filet mignon
  2. Bring to Room Temperature: Let the steak sit out for 20–30 minutes before grilling.
  3. Season the Steak: Generously season both sides with salt and black pepper (or your preferred seasoning).
  4. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to high (around 450–500°F) and clean the grates.
  5. Oil the Grates: Lightly oil the grill grates to prevent sticking.
  6. Grill the Steak: Place the steak on the grill and cook for 3–5 minutes per side depending on thickness and desired doneness.
  7. Check Doneness: Use a meat thermometer (e.g., 130°F for medium-rare) or the finger test for doneness.
  8. Rest the Steak: Remove from heat and let rest for 5–10 minutes to retain juices.
  9. Slice (if needed): Cut against the grain if slicing before serving.
  10. Serve: Plate the steak and enjoy with your favorite sides or sauce.
